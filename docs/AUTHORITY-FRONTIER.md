# The Authority Frontier: verified coordination-free authorization

A one-use approval handed to a fleet of agents cannot be spent twice, even across a network partition, without coordination. And any safe transfer of that authority has to go through a sealed handoff (or a genuine availability gap). Machine-checked in Lean 4, `Crdt/AuthorityFrontier.lean`.

## The claim, in one line

**Your one-shot approval provably cannot double-spend across a partition, and we have machine-checked exactly where coordination must live.**

## What is proven

| Result | Theorem | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Necessity** (the impossibility): safety forces at most one live consumer at any reachable cut, over freely-replicable state (copyable bytes, grow-only receipts) | `authority_frontier_card_le_one` (via `no_disconnected_double_availability`) | Proven, **abstract** `AuthoritySystem`, on `main` |
| **A witnessed double-spend** when coordination is dropped | `double_consume_countermodel` | Proven, on `main` |
| **Sufficiency** (a pattern that works): if the sender seals its own consume before the receiver is enabled, the fleet is safe | `sealed_handoff_safe` | Proven, **finite `CutWorld` model**, on `main` |
| **A third safe shape**: safety can also come from causally sequencing the consumes (the sender's consume poisons the receiver's) | `wSeq_safe` + `wSeq_not_sealed_handoff` | Proven, **finite `CutWorld` model**, on `main` |
| **Sufficiency, lifted** (Layer D): over the *generated* abstract system, all-senders-sealed (every non-receiver domain is never live at any reachable config) implies safety, by structural induction — arbitrary domain type, no finiteness, no enumeration | `sealed_senders_safe` (over `GeneratedAuthoritySystem`) | Proven, **abstract generated system**, on `main` |
| **Why the lift lands one level up, not on the bare system**: a step-free countermodel satisfies the sealed predicate vacuously yet is unsafe, so generation (initial config with empty receipts, reachability generated by induction, live-gated steps) cannot be dropped | `sufficiency_needs_generation` | Proven, **abstract**, on `main` |
| **The lifted predicate still discriminates** (no vacuity, sufficiency not biconditional): an unsafe copy system carrying all the generation laws fails the predicate; an abstract sequencing system is safe with no sealed sender | `copy_not_sealed_senders` + `seq_safe` + `seq_not_sealed_senders` | Proven, **abstract generated system**, on `main` |

## The honest boundaries, stated not buried

- **Necessity is general; sufficiency now stands at the *generated* abstract level (Layer D).** The impossibility is proven for the abstract `AuthoritySystem` (any number of domains). The sufficiency (sealed handoff works) began in the finite `CutWorld` probe model; it now lifts to `GeneratedAuthoritySystem` — the abstract system plus exactly what the finite model supplied for free: an initial config with empty receipts, reachability generated by induction from it, and steps that fire only when live. The lift is honest, not literal. A kernel-checked countermodel (`sufficiency_needs_generation`: a step-free system that satisfies the sealed predicate vacuously yet is unsafe) proves those generation laws cannot be dropped, so the *generated* abstract system is the maximal generality the necessity theorems can be matched at. Over the bare `AuthoritySystem`, reachability is an arbitrary step-closed field with no induction principle and any surviving predicate collapses to `Safe` restated. Still do not round "safe in our model" up to "safe, full stop": the lifted predicate is sufficiency, not a biconditional (`seq_safe` is safe with no sealed sender), and it is not vacuous (`copy_not_sealed_senders` fails it on an unsafe copy system).
- **The safe-transfer taxonomy is richer than two shapes.** The original "handoff or gap" conjecture is incomplete: `wSeq` is a third safe shape (consume-sequencing). Recorded, not hidden.
- **The naive "gap" disjunct is vacuous** (`gap_vacuous`): "there exists a cut where nobody is live" is trivially true at the empty cut, so a `Safe ⟺ handoff∨gap` biconditional would collapse to `Safe ⟺ True`. We prove sufficiency of the sealed handoff and make no biconditional claim.
- **This is a result about the model.** Application to the [seal](https://github.com/velvetmonkey/seal) gate is a separate applicability bridge, and that bridge now exists in [seal-host](https://github.com/velvetmonkey/seal-host) (`Host/AuthorityFrontierBridge.lean`): `AuthoritySystem` instantiated with SealV2's real consume seam, TTL-scoped — within the approval's TTL window, per concurrent replica — an instance mapping, not a refinement of the full validate-and-consume path.
- **No novelty beyond invariant-confluence / escrow-CRDT.** The necessity is a corollary of known distributed-systems theory (Bailis invariant confluence; O'Neil / Balegas escrow), here made machine-checked and given a named abstraction. Not Byzantine, not crash-recovery, not cryptographic.

## Who this is for

Agent fleets, multi-node MCP deployments, offline-first approval flows. The competitor-can't-say claim: a machine-checked boundary on where a distributed approval fabric *must* coordinate to stay single-use, with a witnessed counterexample for what happens when it doesn't.
